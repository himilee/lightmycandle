Light My Candle (LMC) is Non -Governmental Organization established in 2009 by a group of seven young high school graduates. The main purpose was to support children in hardship and difficult condition.

Today, LMC counts thirty (30) active members who provide their support to advance and change lives of vulnerable children. At a glance LMC started with six children beneficiaries who were all in primary school and has gradually been growing to twenty (20) beneficiaries in both primary and secondary schools in Rwanda.

In 2014, LMC opened an office space in Rusororo Sector, Gasabo District, where most of its activities take place. This space does not only serve as an office but also a space where children revise their courses, and can socialize with their peers.

Our Mission

Establish a financial, emotional, and educational support system for vulnerable children and youth that allow them to fulfill their potentials.

Our Vision

Light My Candle envisions a society in which all children can achieve their dreams, regardless of their social or economic status, and grow to be the responsible, confident, caring citizens and leaders of Rwanda’s present and future.

Our Programs

LMC’s current interventions are making a great impact but this doesn’t seem to be fully satisfactory. The coverage is still small and we look forward to working and introducing other programs to benefit our current beneficiaries

Nganirira.Current Program

Nganirira is the first LMC program that takes once every two months at chosen house of one of the beneficiaries. The aim of Nganirira is to bring together all LMC members and beneficiaries to socialize, discuss and bond, create personal connection and inspriration.

In addition, it is an opportunity for LMC committee to evaluate every beneficiary’s environment outside of the classrooms. Beneficiaries get a chance to meet the members of LMC individually so that they can address any particular or personal challenges at their homes.

Umuganda.Current Program

Umuganda is the second LMC program that takes place every last Saturday of the month. LMC members and beneficiaries gather to do community activities, this often includes clearing, cleaning roads premises. It is a way to give back to the community by providing a hand to the needy. The program provides community support that helps communities get together for a good cause and is environmental friendly.

Reading Interventions.Future Program

Reading is key to learning and to acquiring knowledge. Reading opens the reader to the globe and helps to gain confidence. Reading has been a barrier to some of LMC beneficiaries due to a number of factors that include access to books. With this project, LMC beneficiaries will be supported to access quality reading facilities that will help them to improve their reading ability thus positively changing their academic performance.



Establishment of the children library.Future Program

Building and making a library accessible to children will help children to engage in reading activities. The library will be equipped with children/youth age appropriate books that will allow them to continue implanting reading as their day to day business for both their personal development as well as their school performance.

Initiating reading sessions with coaching.Future Program

A team of professionals in reading and/or education for children will be put in place to support children and ensure day to day activities related to reading. This team will make sure that children are provided with reading skills in a way that will motivate children to attend all planned activities as they earn passion and love for reading. We will make sure that we are working hand in hand with other organizations in the country that have already initiated programs to advance reading.

Supporting Children to develop their own books.Future Program

If children given opportunity they can do great things that none would imagine. We want children to be great reader for today as they also pass this from their generation to the others but it is also essential that we start making them the writers for today and for tomorrow. As we build the reading culture, we are also looking forward to preparing the children and youth in writing their own stories and books which will even make them eager to continue gathering writing knowledge by reading and writing own books.
